# HIST 160 Unessay: Power and Inequity in Early America


## What This Is

An interactive data visualization project for HIST 160: Power and Inequity in America to 1865. Instead of a traditional essay, this is a single-page historical exhibit built with HTML and Plotly.js -- exploring the gap between early America's ideals of freedom and the lived reality of Indigenous peoples and enslaved Africans.

## How to Open It

Double-click `index.html` or drag it into any browser. No server needed, no installation. Requires an internet connection once to load Plotly from CDN.

## What's Inside

The exhibit walks through four historical threads:

- **The Promise** -- John Locke's ideals of equality and consent, and who they actually applied to
- **Epidemic** -- smallpox and disease spread after European contact (grounded in Book XII of the Florentine Codex)
- **Enslaved** -- the growth of slavery from 1650 to 1860, annotated at key legislative moments including the SC Slave Code of 1740
- **The Land** -- colonial labor and tribute extraction at household scale (Nahuatl Census, c. 1540)
- **Resistance** -- Mum Bett's freedom suit and Frederick Douglass's Fourth of July speech

## Charts

1. Bar chart: 1790 U.S. population broken down by legal/political status
2. Scattergeo map: epidemic contact points across North America and Mesoamerica
3. Line chart: enslaved population growth 1650-1860 with legislative annotations
4. Scattergeo map: colonial, Indigenous, slavery, and resistance sites

## Sources

Every caption is tagged `[Course Reading]` or `[Outside]` so it's clear what comes from assigned texts vs. external data. Primary source quotes are cited directly; paraphrases are labeled as such.
